The Foundation

A Centre for Legal Excellence

National Law University Delhi (NLU Delhi) stands as a premier institution dedicated to the advancement of legal education, research, and public policy. Established with a mandate to foster ethical leadership and social responsibility, the University’s pedagogy seamlessly integrates law with economics, finance, and technology.

The Initiative

PGIP

The PGIP is NLU Delhi’s flagship intervention in the corporate sphere. Conceptualized to bridge the gap between academic theory and the practical exigencies of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code (IBC), this programme sits at the critical intersection of law, forensic accounting, and corporate restructuring.
Academic Framework

LL.M. / M.A. in Insolvency

Delivered under the aegis of the Insolvency and Bankruptcy Board of India (IBBI), the curriculum ensures complete alignment with regulatory standards, covering advanced corporate insolvency resolution and distressed asset management.

The Cohort

Cross-Disciplinary Synergy

The classroom is a melting pot of professional expertise, comprising lawyers, CAs, CSs, and cost accountants. This diversity fosters a collaborative environment where legal interpretation meets financial analysis.

The Outcome

Strategic Leadership

Utilizing a two-year strategic model with immersive practical training, NLU Delhi nurtures competent, ethical professionals ready to assume leadership roles within regulatory bodies and top-tier law firms.

Justice S.K. Kaul

Former Judge, Supreme Court of India

Justice Sanjay Kishan Kaul is a distinguished jurist and former Judge of the Supreme Court of India, whose legal career spans over four decades.

He earned his LL.B. from the Campus Law Centre, University of Delhi, in 1982 and subsequently enrolled as an Advocate with the Bar Council of Delhi.

Throughout his practice, Justice Kaul appeared extensively in commercial, civil, writ, original and company matters in both the Delhi High Court and the Supreme Court of India.

He served as an Advocate-on-Record at the Supreme Court from 1987 to 1999, was designated a Senior Advocate in 1999, and acted as Senior Counsel for prestigious institutions including the Delhi High Court, Delhi University and the Delhi Development Authority. Elevated to the bench in 2001, he served as a Judge of the Delhi High Court, before becoming Chief Justice of the Punjab and Haryana High Court in 2013 and Chief Justice of the Madras High Court in 2014. In 2017, he was appointed as a Judge of the Supreme Court of India, where he contributed significantly to the jurisprudence on constitutional law, civil liberties, and commercial disputes until his retirement in December 2023. Justice Kaul’s commitment to legal excellence and judicial integrity makes him a valued member of the academic community.
